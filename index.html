The Story

The story follows Link, a young hero, who is called upon by Princess Zelda to embark on a quest to stop the evil Ganondorf and save the kingdom of Hyrule. Link must collect three spiritual stones and the Ocarina of Time to access the Sacred Realm. However, his accidental action in opening the Door of Time allows Ganondorf to enter the Sacred Realm and corrupt it.

Link is sealed away for seven years, aging from a child to an adult. He awakens as the "Chosen One," destined to confront Ganondorf. He must awaken the Sages and gather the Spiritual Stones to face Ganondorf.

The plot revolves around Link's journey through Hyrule, solving puzzles and completing dungeons, and manipulating time with the Ocarina of Time. Link acquires various skills and magical items to battle enemies and overcome obstacles.

Ultimately, Link assembles the Sages, reclaims the Master Sword, and faces Ganondorf, who has transformed into Ganon, an evil being. After an epic battle, Link rescues Zelda and restores peace to Hyrule.

The Triangle of the Goddesses

The Triforce, a powerful relic, is said to be created by the three Golden Goddesses who shaped the world of Hyrule.

Din, the Goddess of Power, shaped the land of Hyrule and created the physical world.

Nayru, the Goddess of Wisdom, gave order and knowledge to the world, ensuring that it followed the laws of nature.

Farore, the Goddess of Courage, breathed life into the world, creating the diverse creatures that inhabit it.

Together, the three Golden Goddesses left behind the Triforce, a powerful triangle with each piece representing one of their attributes: Power, Wisdom, and Courage. The Triforce was placed in the Sacred Realm, a parallel dimension accessible only through the Temple of Time. Whoever could unite the three pieces of the Triforce would be granted incredible power and the ability to shape reality.

Explore Hyrule!

  • Hyrule: The fictional kingdom where most of the game takes place. Hyrule is a land of lush forests, open fields, mountains, and deserts. It's a vibrant and diverse world with a deep history and a mix of friendly and hostile inhabitants.
  • Time Travel: One of the game's central mechanics is time travel. Link, the protagonist, can transition between two time periods: his childhood and adulthood. This mechanic influences the state of the world, the locations he can access, and the characters he interacts with.
  • Sages and Temples: To combat the evil forces of Ganondorf, Link must awaken and rescue the six Sages from their respective temples. Each temple corresponds to a different elemental theme and poses unique challenges.
  • Races and Locations:
    • Kokiri Forest: Home to the Kokiri, a race of childlike forest dwellers.
    • Goron City: A settlement inhabited by the Gorons, a rock-eating race.
    • Zora's Domain: The domain of the aquatic Zoras.
    • Gerudo Desert: A harsh desert region ruled by the Gerudo, a group of female thieves.
    • Kakariko Village: A peaceful village at the base of Death Mountain, inhabited by the Sheikah and other races.
  • Hyrule Castle: The residence of Princess Zelda and an important location in the game.
  • Temple of Time: The Temple of Time is a crucial location where Link acquires the Master Sword, and it serves as the gateway between his two life stages.

The Ocarina of Time

The Ocarina of Time is a small, flute-like musical instrument. It has six holes for the player to cover, allowing it to produce different notes and melodies. The ocarina is made of a unique material known as the "Deku Tree's bark."

The Ocarina has the power to manipulate time. By playing specific melodies on the Ocarina, the user can travel between two time periods: the past and the future.

The Ocarina of Time can play a variety of songs, each with a unique effect. Some of the most well-known songs in the game include the Song of Time, the Song of Storms, and the Song of Healing.

The Boy From The Forest

Link is initially a young boy who lives among the Kokiri in Kokiri Forest. He is known as the "Hero of Time." He is chosen by destiny to wield the Master Sword and become the hero who can save Hyrule.

Link develops strong bonds with other characters, including Saria, a friend from Kokiri Forest, and Princess Zelda, with whom he shares a deep connection. He forms alliances with characters like Darunia (Goron leader), Ruto (Zora princess), and Nabooru (Gerudo warrior).

Link is the central figure in the struggle against Ganondorf and plays a crucial role in awakening the Sages, who are instrumental in sealing Ganondorf away.

Link's courage, determination, and selflessness make him a true hero, living up to his title as the "Hero of Time." He is known for his iconic green tunic and hat, as well as his skill with a sword and shield.

The Princess

Zelda is the princess of the Kingdom of Hyrule, and as such, she holds a position of great importance and responsibility. She is the heir to the Hylian throne.

She is known for her wisdom, which is reflected in her possession of the Triforce of Wisdom, a sacred artifact in the game's world. Her wisdom guides her actions and decisions throughout the game.

In the game, Zelda demonstrates courage and resourcefulness when she decides to take on the identity of Sheik to protect herself from Ganondorf. This courage allows her to aid Link in his quest without falling into Ganondorf's clutches.

Zelda's connection to the Ocarina of Time, a magical musical instrument, is a significant aspect of her character. She teaches Link various songs on the ocarina, which are essential for solving puzzles, progressing through the game, and even time travel.

Throughout the game, Princess Zelda undergoes character growth, evolving from a royal princess into a more proactive and resourceful character who takes direct action to protect her kingdom.

The King of Thieves

Ganondorf is a Gerudo, a desert-dwelling tribe of thieves, in the land of Hyrule. He is born into the Gerudo race, which is primarily comprised of female members, and he is the first male Gerudo born in a hundred years. His birth is seen as an omen by the Gerudo, and he is destined for great power and ambition.

Ganondorf is driven by a deep desire for power and domination. He seeks to conquer Hyrule and obtain the legendary Triforce, a divine relic that grants immense power and the ability to grant any wish. His lust for power and his evil intentions set him on a path of conquest.

Ganondorf initially poses as an ally, forming an alliance with the royal family of Hyrule. He manipulates Princess Zelda's visions and tricks Link, the game's protagonist, into revealing the entrance to the Sacred Realm. This allows him to access the Triforce.

Ganondorf's lust for power and his evil intentions ultimately lead to his transformation into Ganon, a monstrous and boar-like creature with immense physical strength and magical abilities.
